Service Name: What is PSID

What is PSID:
PSID (Payment Slip ID) is a unique 24-digit number generated by Paymir, a digital payment gateway of KPITB’s FinTech Unit. It identifies specific payment transactions and is part of Pakistan’s 1LINK system, enabling secure digital payments for fees, taxes, bills, and other charges.

=== Service Start ===
Service Name: How to Generate PSID

How to Generate a PSID:
A PSID is generated by Paymir (KPITB’s FinTech Unit) through the institution or department you need to pay. You don’t create it yourself, it is issued automatically after you complete your application or registration.

Service Name: PSID Format and Details

PSID Number Format:
A PSID (Payment Slip ID) is a 24-digit unique numeric identifier assigned to each payment transaction. It is sometimes written without spaces and sometimes in groups of digits, but it is always 24 digits long.

Example PSID format: 10121111418142611121001

Service Name: PSID Payment Security

Is PSID Payment Secure:
Yes. PSID-based payments are processed through 1LINK, Pakistan's national interbank payment switch, which uses industry-standard encryption and security protocols. Payments made via Easypaisa, JazzCash, and major banks are equally secure.

Service Name: PSID Payment Troubleshooting

Common PSID Payment Issues and Solutions:

Issue 1: Payment failed but money was deducted
- Wait 24 hours - most failed transactions are automatically reversed
- Check your bank statement or app transaction history for reversal confirmation
- If not reversed after 24 hours, contact your bank's customer support with the transaction reference number

Issue 2: PSID shows as already paid but I have not paid
- Someone else may have paid on your behalf, or there may be a system error
- Contact the issuing institution with your PSID to verify payment records
- If unauthorized payment was made, file a complaint with the institution

Issue 3: PSID expired before I could pay
- Log back into the issuing institution's portal
- Check if the PSID can be renewed or if you need to generate a new one
- Some institutions automatically generate a new PSID upon expiry

Issue 4: PSID not accepted in the app
- Ensure you are entering exactly 24 digits - no spaces, no extra characters
- Confirm you are using the correct payment channel (some PSIDs only work on specific platforms)
- Try a different payment channel (e.g., if Easypaisa fails, try JazzCash or internet banking)

Issue 5: Payment confirmation not received
- Check your mobile number and email registered with the institution
- Check your spam/junk folder for email confirmation
- Payment confirmation is also visible in your bank app transaction history
- Contact the issuing institution with your transaction reference
